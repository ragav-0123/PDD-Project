#### 1. Attack on Titan
User Query:  
- "Tell me about Attack on Titan."  
- "What is Attack on Titan about?"  
- "Who are the main characters in Attack on Titan?"  

Answer:  
Attack on Titan (Shingeki no Kyojin) is a Japanese anime series set in a world where humanity lives inside cities surrounded by enormous walls to protect themselves from Titans, giant humanoid creatures who devour humans. The story follows Eren Yeager, Mikasa Ackerman, and Armin Arlert as they join the fight against the Titans after their hometown is destroyed.

# Understanding Anime Content Types: Canon, Filler, and Mixed Content

## Canon Content
Canon content refers to storylines and events that are directly adapted from the original source material (usually manga or light novels). These episodes:
- Stay true to the original creator's vision and narrative
- Advance the main plot of the series
- Develop important character relationships and growth
- Introduce key plot elements that impact future story arcs
- Are considered official and essential to understanding the complete story

Example: In "Naruto," episodes that follow Naruto's journey to become a ninja and his battles with the Akatsuki organization as depicted in the manga are canon content.

## Filler Content
Filler content consists of original episodes or story arcs created specifically for the anime that weren't present in the source material. These episodes:
- Don't impact the main storyline
- Often feature side adventures or alternative scenarios
- May explore secondary characters or relationships
- Can be skipped without losing understanding of the main plot
- Are typically created to give the source material time to progress

Example: In "Bleach," the Bount Arc is entirely filler content, created solely for the anime and not present in the original manga.

## Mixed Canon/Filler Content
Mixed content combines elements from both canon and filler material in the same episode or story arc. These episodes:
- Include some events from the original source material
- Add extra scenes, dialogues, or mini-plots not in the source
- May expand on canon events with additional details
- Often bridge gaps between canon story arcs
- Can provide additional context or character development

Example: In "One Piece," some episodes might follow the manga's main plot points but add extra scenes or extend fights beyond their original length to fill the episode time.

## Impact on Viewing Experience
Understanding these content types helps viewers:
1. Decide which episodes to watch based on their preferences
2. Follow the main story more effectively
3. Distinguish between original creator content and anime studio additions
4. Make informed decisions about episode selection when time is limited
5. Appreciate both the source material and anime-original content

## Common Indicators of Content Type
Viewers can often identify content types by:
- Checking episode guides and fan wikis
- Noticing shifts in animation style or quality
- Observing whether the episode advances the main plot
- Recognizing when characters act differently from their established personalities
- Identifying standalone episodes that don't connect to the larger narrative
